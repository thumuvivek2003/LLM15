Road Bike Specifications:

The AeroPro X1 is a lightweight road bike designed for speed and endurance. 
It features a carbon fiber frame weighing just 7.5 kilograms, making it one of the lightest in its class. 
The bike includes Shimano Ultegra R8000 components and hydraulic disc brakes for consistent performance. 
Its aerodynamic design reduces drag by 12% compared to previous models. 

Battery-Assisted Commuter Bike:

The CityRide E-Commuter 400 features a removable 500Wh lithium battery providing a range of up to 90 kilometers on a single charge. 
It weighs 18.2 kilograms including the battery. 
The bike supports pedal assist with five levels and a top assisted speed of 25 km/h. 
Charging time is approximately 4.5 hours using the included 2A charger.

Mountain Bike Details:

The TrailMaster 900 uses an aluminum alloy frame and front suspension fork for rough terrain handling. 
It weighs 13.8 kilograms and comes with 29-inch tires for improved stability. 
The gearing system is a 12-speed SRAM Eagle drivetrain suitable for steep climbs and descents. 

Bike Comparison Summary:

Among the three models, the AeroPro X1 is the lightest and best suited for racing. 
The CityRide E-Commuter provides the longest range due to its electric assist. 
The TrailMaster 900 offers the best off-road performance. 
All three models feature ergonomic saddles and adjustable handlebars.
